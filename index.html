The Quiet Champion

Steve Yzerman

A Timeline of Steve Yzerman

  • May 9th, 1965

    Steve Yzerman was born in Cranbrook, British Columbia

  • 1977

    As a youngster, Steve played in the Quebec International Pee-Wee Hockey Tournament

  • 1980-81

    Played for the Nepean Raiders in the CJHL

  • 1981

    Drafted by the Peterborough Petes in the OHL

  • 1981-83

    Played in the OHL for the Peterborough Petes

  • 1983

    Drafted 4th overall by the Detroit Red Wings

  • 1983-84

    Made his rookie season debut in the NHL for the Detroit Red Wings

  • 1986

    Steve Yzerman was named captain of the Detroit Red Wings

  • 1987-88

    Scored 50 goals and finished with 102 points

  • 1987-93

    Hit the 100 points or more mark 6 seasons in a row

  • 1994-95

    Led the Detroit Red Wings to the Stanley Cup finals but lost

  • 1995-96

    Scored an incredible game 7 overtime goal against the St. Louis Blues after stealing the puck from Wayne Gretzky

  • 1996-97

    Quieted all critics as he led Detroit to their first Stanley Cup championship in 42 years

  • 1997-98

    Won the Stanley Cup again and was awarded the Conn Smythe Trophy

  • 1999

    Became the 11th player in NHL history to score 600 goals

  • 2001-02

    Even with a severe knee injury, Yzerman played and led his team to their 3rd Stanley Cup as their captain

  • 2002-03

    Underwent knee realignment surgery missing 61 games but came back and won the Bill Masterton Memorial Trophy for perseverance

  • 2004

    Yzerman was hit in the eye by a puck and broke his orbital bone and scratched his cornea

  • 2005

    Yzerman signs his last player contract

  • May 1, 2006

    Yzerman played his last game in the NHL

  • July 3rd, 2006

    Steve Yzerman announces his retirement from the NHL

  • Jan 2nd, 2007

    The Detroit Red Wings retire Yzerman's jersey number 19 and was presented the key to the city of Detroit. This day was also declared as "Steve Yzerman Day" in Michigan.

  • June 23, 2009

    Steve Yzerman is inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame

  • May 25, 2010

    The Tampa Bay Lightning hire Steve Yzerman as their new General Manager

  • June 24, 2015

    The General Manager of the Year Award is won by Steve Yzerman

  • April 19, 2019

    Fittingly, on April 19th in the year 2019, Steve Yzerman returns home to Detroit as the General Manager of the Detroit Red Wings
